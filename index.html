The Master Sculptor

Completing Nature's Work

"I firmly believe that my work is not the creation of something new, but the completion of something that nature would have carved during that piece of wood or tree's life time."

Boniface Chikwenhere is a Zimbabwean-born sculptor, woodcarver, and artist based in Cape Town, South Africa. Coming from a rich family tradition spanning three generations of abstract wood sculpting, Boniface developed an early eye for identifying forms hidden in the natural contours of wood.

His main responsibility is to connect with each specimen he finds, bringing out the hidden shape lying beneath the surface of the wood. The resulting works are polished or oiled depending on each species, showing off the magnificence of the wood with its natural colors, patterns, and grains.

Sustainable Sourcing

The Woods of Southern Africa

No new wood is ever harvested. Boniface exclusively uses ancient specimens that have been dead for decades, if not centuries, sourced through a network spanning Zimbabwe, Namibia, Malawi, and South Africa.

Limpopo & Zimbabwe

Mopane

A heavy, dense African hardwood known for its rich, warm reddish-brown hues and striking straw-colored sapwood. Highly resistant, it provides incredible structural contrast in abstract carvings.

  • Texture: Extremely hard, fine grain
  • Colors: Dark red, dark brown, golden straw
Kruger & Lowveld

Leadwood

One of Africa's heaviest woods, so dense that it sinks in water. When dead trees stand for decades, the wood turns a dark, mineralized grey-black, producing museum-grade textures and weight.

  • Texture: Glass-like polish when worked
  • Colors: Deep charcoal grey, rich black-brown
Eastern & Southern Cape

Sneezewood

Famously durable wood with a distinct aromatic quality. Its dense golden-brown grain contains natural oils that give finished sculptures a gorgeous, natural satin sheen.

  • Texture: Aromatic, waxy feel
  • Colors: Light golden brown, copper orange
Dry Savannahs

Wild Olive

Prized for its beautifully figured, wavy grain lines and contrasting light and dark bands. Boniface shapes these to emphasize the muscular contours of human and wildlife forms.

  • Texture: Smooth, wavy swirling grain
  • Colors: Golden yellow with deep brown figures

Safety & Treatment Guarantee

Every single piece of driftwood and mineralized root collected is thoroughly cleaned, treated, and fumigated in Cape Town before carving begins, ensuring it safely passes international customs and remains preserved for generations.

Behind the Art

The Journey of a Sculpture

How raw wood, weathered by nature for hundreds of years, becomes a completed abstract masterpiece.

01

Sourcing

Local communities across Southern Africa search riverbeds, swamps, and forests to locate ancient tree roots and weathered driftwood, generating a sustainable income.

02

Fumigation & Cleaning

Each collected piece is brought to the Cape Town workshop, thoroughly scrubbed to remove dirt/sand, treated, and fumigated to ensure preservation and compliance with global shipping laws.

03

Connection

Boniface spends days studying the raw wood. He observes the grains, cracks, and shapes left by decades of erosion to discover the creature or form sleeping inside.

04

Completion

Using chisels and files, Boniface carves only what is necessary to define the form. The sculpture is then polished or oiled, showing off the natural grains and warm colors of the wood.
